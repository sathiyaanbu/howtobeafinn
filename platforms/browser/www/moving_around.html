Cheapest way to travel between cities are buses.

Trains are faster but more expensive.

There are cheap carpool options available in internet.

Currently popular and easy option is Facebooks different carpool groups.

Trips from Jyväskylä to Helsinki cost about 15 euros.

Hitch hiking in Finland? 1/2
It’s very safe to hitch hike in Finland but it’s not that easy.

Few reasons: there are not many cars and people are a bit afraid to pick up stranger (although they would like to), the weather is usually bad.

Hitchhiking in Finland? 2/2

But if you choose to try it, best option is to head for big gas stations outside city center (usually ABC).

This works better than just standing on the road.

Traveling with your own car?

Roads are in good shape and there isn’t hardly ever any traffic.
So traveling with your own car is fast.

Speed limits in side city area are usually around 40-50 km/h and on the highway between 80-120.

During winter the limits are lower for safety reasons.

Child seat is mandatory in cars by the law.

Bicycles are great way getting around in city.

Finland is no where near Denmark but it’s getting more and more bike-friendly.

So first thing to do is to find a cheap bikeshop.
