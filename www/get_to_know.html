Stereotype of a Finn is silent introvert that’s keeping their head down.
Maybe because of the cold or the shame. This is only half the truth.

Finns can be open, friendly and very humorous.
They just need some more time to get started and get to know you. Or booze. That will change everything.

Personal space-
Finns do love their personal space.
In fact, they would rather stand while riding a bus than sit with another person close to them.

No small talk.
Finns place great value on words, which is reflected in the tendency to say little and avoid ‘unnecessary’ small talk.

Mean what you say!
If you promise something to a Finn, you should do that with no excuse, or you will lose person's respect.

They may seem shy!

Finns don’t talk that much and don’t take contact to outsiders by themselves.

But be active, you may be surprised Even they may seem shy, they actually like to talk and help foreigners.

Finns are law-abinding.

If the traffic light for pedestrians is red but there are no cars, Finns still wait for the green light.

Finns stick to rules and laws. They are the ’Apt pupils of EU’ (a term that is sometimes used in not so positive manner)

So if you don’t want to stick out, wait for the light. How stupid that might feel like.
