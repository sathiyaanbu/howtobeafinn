If you want to taste local foods, best option is lunch time in restaurants or canteens in workplaces.

Remember to add spices!

Finnish food is generally rather tasteless. So you better add salt and spices.

Porridge for breakfast

For breakfast people have usually either porridge (oat, rye) or rye bread and coffee. Plenty of coffee!

Coffee

Did you know that Finns are in first place of coffee consumption in the world with 9,6 kg per capita?
Consumption of 2,64 cups/day

Peasoup and pancakes on thursdays
During the war the Finnish army was fed with pea soup (hernekeitto).
Many restaurants continues the tradition of serving pea soup accompanied by pancakes (pannukakku) for lunch every Thursday.

You should always eat seasonal food, imported or domestic.
Satokausikalenteri tells you which fruits and vegetables are currently best for this season.

Finns eat lot of root vegetables like potato, turnip and beetroot.
The season is short so lot of vegetables are imported. The selection in supermarkets nowadays is very good.

Pizza, pizza, pizza

There are plenty of pizzerias in Finland. Cost per pizza is around 5-10 euros.
You can either choose between Kotipizza (big Finnish franchise) or one of the small independent pizzerias.
